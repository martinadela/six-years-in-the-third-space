The mythical Gurgel Itaipu E150 was not only the first electric car in Brazil, but the first of its kind in the region, which fueled a supposed "future" towards a new energy matrix, proposing an "environmentally friendly" model. Originally presented at the Salão do Automóvel de São Paulo in 1974, the Itaipu failed a short time later and was displaced by the 'Programa Nacional do Álcool' of the time, which led Brazil - until today - to a mobility model based on the production of sugar cane. This productive model has transformed “high biodiversity” regions into commodities, cloaking extractive capitalism with a “green” label.
