Six years. A lot has started, a lot has ended, and a lot has changed.

Six years in (the) Third Space: learning better ways to host ideas, engage with people, and promote fairer cultural practices.

Six years in (the) Third Space is just the beginning, we hope.

Similarly to an interplanetary space full of satellites of different dimensions and speeds, routes and missions, the art scene is a constellation of varying projects, operators, and spaces coexisting and rotating around each other: sometimes coming together and at other times traveling outward towards their own destinations ⎼still ultimately linked to one another by a common scope of purpose. In their own means, all aim at improving the conditions of and for art and culture in society. Simultaneously, and along with the sociocultural course at large, the global contemporary art field is becoming increasingly capitalized and art practices are highly individualized. Embraced especially by the western thought (ostensibly guarding individual freedom), knowledge, people, and other beings, objects, and ways of doing and being are assessed by their commercial value, efficiency, measurability, homogeneity, and normativity. That is: their controllability. In the violent responses to the recent international movements for human rights and equality, and in the midst of the global pandemic, it has yet again become evident that societal structures are largely built indeed to maintain systemic oppression.

Based in Helsinki, Finland, Third Space strives to have an impact on the local situation by promoting collaborative practices over competitive ones. A cross-border artist collective and a long-term cultural project, Third Space has been for over six years now a nest for inclusive art practices. Our role, most importantly, has been to facilitate a space for encounters, a space for people, a space for dialogue through art, and its production ⎼ while acknowledging the inevitable disagreements, challenges, and conflicts. Third Space has provided self-funded, free-of-charge space that has welcomed multidisciplinary projects and open discussions on sociopolitical issues and contexts. Since December 2013, we have hosted 305 events and had a chance to collaborate and co-organize with 608 people including artists, curators, researchers, and art educators. Working through our diverse geopolitical views and backgrounds, we have been practicing collaboration and reflecting on its meanings: listening, sharing, (un)learning, and also accepting the complexities and challenges that come along with collective work. As a project in ongoing transformation, Third Space would not be what it is now without the work of everyone who has inhabited it and contributed to its shaping.

For our sixth anniversary, we launch this virtual publication that specifically tackles the question of (and sets examples on) how to share and negotiate the space for collaboration. "Six Years in the Third Space" celebrates working and doing together as a plural, open and horizontal space for cultural dialogue: a practice of empathy and exchange. The publication compiles the valuable work of artists and cultural workers, institutions, and non-institutions who we have been working with through the years, or hope to work with in the future. The featured contributors were invited to reflect on the various aspects of collaboration from the perspective of their own practice. We also proposed the entries to consider conceptual lenses such as time/space, community, failure, lines/borders, theory/action, encounter/dis-encounter, and host/guest.

Over these six years, the journey of Third Space has been shaped by the collective efforts of nineteen people including former members. For this anniversary we want to warmly thank you all for contributing to the growth of Third Space: Daniel Malpica (poet and graphic designer), Eva Karhu (photographer and sculptor), Ahmed Al-Nawas (curator and artist), Christopher Wessels (filmmaker and hustler), Juan Duarte Regino (media artist and researcher), Selina Väliheikki (curator), Ali Akbar Mehta (transmedia artist, curator, and researcher), Steve Maher (visual and relational artist), Sébastien Piquemal (computer engineer and explorer of the artistic capabilities of machines), Marianne Savallampi (curator), Raine Vasquez (artist and writer), Jaime Culebro (photographer), Elham Rahmati (visual artist and curator), and Ramina Habibollah (multidisciplinary artist and curator).

We will continue working for the now and towards the fluid future. Third Space may become many yet unimaginable things… a new type of collective space, an activism site, a kitchen, a social and political sphere or a communal center hosting people and beings; moving ways and moving needs, art, utopias, and ideologies, dreams and ideas.
