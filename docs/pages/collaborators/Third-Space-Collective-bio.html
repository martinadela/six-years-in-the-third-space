Sepideh Rahaa (Iran) is a multidisciplinary artist, researcher and educator based in Helsinki. Through her practice she actively investigates and questions prevailing power structures, social norms and conventions while focusing on womanhood, storytelling and everyday resistances. Currently she is pursuing her doctoral studies in Contemporary Art at Aalto University. Her research interests are representation in contemporary art, silenced histories, decolonisation, feminist politics and intersectionality, collaboration and global social justice. Her aim is to initiate methods within contemporary art for creating spaces for dialogue. Since 2015, Rahaa actively works as the collective member at Third Space.

Rosa María Sánchez Hernández better known as Rosamaría Bolom (Mexico/Finland). Psychologist and Sculptor of dreams on paper. Her artistic practice and work is related to collectivity, symbolic language, otherness, subjectivity, human relationships, identity, immigration, feminism, pedagogical tools and human rights. Her artworks include poetry, paintings, drawings, engravings, performance, photos and paper sculpture. As an artist Rosamaría is convinced that the universal language comes from the soul and is the best weapon to combat misunderstanding, prejudices, racism, ignorance, fear and other contemporary ills. Her constant work in collective processes has led Rosamaría to be part of collectives and projects that contribute to the cultural-artistic scene in Finland. From 2013 she has been involved at Third Space Collective as a co-founder and active member.  

Martina Miño Pérez (Quito, Ecuador, 1990) is a visual artist, writer, cultural manager and cook who currently works between Helsinki, Quito and Berlin. Martina did her masters in Visual Culture, Curating and Contemporary Art at Aalto University of Arts, Design and Architecture in Finland and focused her thesis on The Sense of Taste and the Act of Eating as Metaphorical Tools. At the moment she creates edible installations that explore the various ways in which food functions as a symbolic device in contemporary art. Her research investigates domesticity as a sphere of radical thought, and the body as a tool for sensitive interpretation. Martina has been part of Third Space Collective since 2016.

Elina Nissinen​​ is a visual artist working mainly by painting and ceramics. Currently pursuing an MFA degree in the Academy of Fine Arts, Uniarts, her interests revolve around the idea of the unknown/unknowable, an attempt to get lost, and rediscovering the wonder of fantasizing. She is also curious, e.g., about the concepts of presence & absence, the point where image and object cling onto each other, and the question of what distinguishes meaning from meaninglessness. Elina’s collaborative projects explore (alternative) narratives, beliefs, collective anxieties, and coping methods embedded in Western contemporary culture - these themes have an echo in her solo work, too. Elina joined the Third Space collective in November 2017.

Ana Gutieszca ​​ (Mexico) is a visual and sound artist born in the dry lands of the northern Mexican plateau. Drawing is the core of her work. By addressing the question of the animal and its ambivalent presence through symbolic thinking, she embraces the otherness. With a background in visual arts and music, she sculpts the unfathomable boundaries of drawing through the creation of analog instruments, the sonification of graphite and its deconstruction into performance art. Her music is rough as the desert, combining hypnotic brain-melting noise from self-made analogs and blistering broken techno beats from drum machines. She is also known for being a founder member of Third Space, where she actively curates the Sound Room concert series.
